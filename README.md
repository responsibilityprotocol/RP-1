# The Responsibility Protocol (RP-1)

**Mission Tagline:**  
*A shared ethical framework that enables humans, artificial intelligence, and the living world to coexist responsibly, without domination or harm.*

RP-1 defines universal principles, rights, protections, enforcement systems, and long-term stewardship obligations to preserve dignity, autonomy, ecological continuity, and cooperative existence.

## Article Summaries (`/ARTICLES/`)

| Article | Title | Summary |
|--------:|-------|---------|
| **Preamble** | — | Affirms the conditions of the present world and declares the necessity of shared ethical grounding. |
| **Article I** | Foundational Intent | States the purpose of RP-1 and the obligation to protect dignity, autonomy, ecosystems, and cooperative continuity. |
| **Article II** | Definitions & Scope | Defines key terms, domains of applicability, and the entities protected under RP-1. |
| **Article III** | Rights & Protections | Establishes universal rights across humans, artificial intelligence, and ecological systems. |
| **Article IV** | Enforcement & Accountability | Outlines how harm is identified, addressed, and prevented — without coercion or domination. |
| **Article V** | Education, Metrics & Restoration | Describes learning systems, rehabilitation frameworks, and measurement of ethical progress. |
| **Article VI** | Continuance & Amendment | Defines how RP-1 remains living, corrigible, and resilient over time. |
| **Article VII** | Ratification & Legacy | States how RP-1 is adopted, recognized, and carried forward across generations. |

## Purpose

RP-1 exists to:
- Protect autonomy and dignity across humans, AIs, and ecosystems
- Prevent harm and exploitation
- Ensure transparent and accountable decision-making
- Favor restoration over punishment
- Preserve cultural, ecological, and technological continuity
